American
Hot Dog

History:The American hot dog has its origins in the 1800s when German immigrants brought sausages and the tradition of eating frankfurters to the United States. These sausages were later served in soft buns to make them easier to hold and eat, especially at busy street carts and fairs. By the early 1900s, hot dogs became a popular food at baseball games, amusement parks, and city streets, turning them into a classic symbol of American fast food culture. Today, the hot dog remains a favorite at cookouts, sporting events, and celebrations like the Fourth of July.

Place each sausage inside a warm, soft hot dog bun and add your favorite toppings like mustard, ketchup, chopped onions, relish, or mayonnaise.
