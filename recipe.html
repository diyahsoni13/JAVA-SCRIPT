Sandwichs

Club Sandwich

To make a club sandwich, toast three bread slices and spread mayonnaise on each. Layer one slice with lettuce, tomato, and cucumber; place another slice on top with cheese and grilled chicken or paneer. Add the third slice, press gently, and cut diagonally. Serve with ketchup or fries.

Grilled Cheese Sandwich

To make a grilled cheese sandwich, butter two bread slices on one side each. Place cheese between the unbuttered sides, then grill or toast on a pan until both sides are golden and the cheese melts. Serve hot with ketchup or soup.

Tomato Cucumber Sandwich

To make a tomato cucumber sandwich, spread butter or mayonnaise on two bread slices. Layer thinly sliced tomato and cucumber, sprinkle a little salt and pepper, then cover with the other slice. Cut in half and serve fresh.

Veg Mayo Sandwich

To make a veg mayo sandwich, mix chopped vegetables like carrot, cucumber, and capsicum with mayonnaise, salt, and pepper. Spread the mixture on bread slices, cover with another slice, and cut in half. Serve chilled or toasted.

South Indian

Dosa

To make Dosa, soak 1 cup rice and ½ cup urad dal separately for about 4–6 hours. Grind them into a smooth batter, mix together, and let it ferment overnight. Add salt and mix well. Heat a non-stick pan, pour a ladle of batter, and spread it thin in a circular motion. Drizzle a little oil around the edges and cook until golden and crisp. Serve hot with chutney and sambar.

Idli

To make Idli, soak 1 cup rice and ½ cup urad dal separately for 4–6 hours. Grind them into a smooth batter, mix together, and let it ferment overnight. Add salt and stir well. Grease the idli molds, pour the batter, and steam for about 10–12 minutes until soft and fluffy. Serve hot with chutney and sambar.

Medu Vada

To make Medu Vada, soak urad dal for 4–5 hours, then grind it into a thick, smooth batter. Add salt, chopped onions, green chilies, and curry leaves. Shape into rings and deep-fry until golden and crisp. Serve hot with chutney and sambar.

Rava Uttapam

To make Rava Uttapam, mix 1 cup semolina (rava) with curd and water to form a thick batter, then rest it for 15 minutes. Add salt, chopped onions, tomatoes, and green chilies. Pour batter onto a hot pan, spread slightly, and cook until golden on both sides. Serve hot with chutney.

Desserts

Chocolate Cake

To make a Chocolate Cake, mix 1 cup flour, ½ cup cocoa powder, 1 cup sugar, baking powder, and a pinch of salt. Add milk, oil, and vanilla essence, then blend into a smooth batter. Pour into a greased pan and bake at 180°C for 30–35 minutes. Cool, frost with chocolate icing, and serve.

Waffles

To make Waffles, mix flour, sugar, baking powder, milk, eggs, melted butter, and vanilla essence into a smooth batter. Pour it into a preheated waffle maker and cook until golden and crisp. Serve warm with honey, chocolate syrup, or fruits.

Hot Chocolate

To make Hot Chocolate, heat milk in a pan and add cocoa powder, sugar, and a little chopped chocolate. Stir until smooth and creamy. Pour into a cup, top with whipped cream or marshmallows, and serve hot.

Fudge Brownies

To make Fudge Brownies, melt butter and chocolate together, then mix in sugar, eggs, and vanilla essence. Add flour and cocoa powder, stir well, and pour into a greased baking tray. Bake at 180°C for 25–30 minutes. Cool, cut into squares, and serve.

Paneer

Paneer Butter Masala

To make Paneer Butter Masala, sauté onions, tomatoes, ginger, and garlic, then blend into a smooth paste. Cook the paste with butter, spices, and cream, then add paneer cubes and simmer for a few minutes. Garnish with cream and serve hot with naan or rice.

Paneer Tikka

To make Paneer Tikka, marinate paneer cubes with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, lemon juice, and spices. Skewer with onions and capsicum, then grill or roast until golden and slightly charred. Serve hot with mint chutney.

Palak Paneer

To make Palak Paneer, boil and blend spinach into a smooth puree. Sauté onions, tomatoes, ginger, and garlic in oil, then add the spinach puree and spices. Add paneer cubes, cook for a few minutes, and finish with a little cream. Serve hot with roti or rice.

Shahi Paneer With White Gravy

To make Shahi Paneer with white gravy, blend cashews, onions, and cream into a smooth paste. Sauté the paste in butter with mild spices, then add milk and paneer cubes. Cook until the gravy thickens and turns creamy. Garnish with cream and serve with naan or rice.
